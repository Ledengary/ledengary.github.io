Research Papers

Leveraging Deep Reinforcement Learning in Gender Classification of Fetal Heartbeat Sounds

A collaboration with MM. Ghassemi, T. Alhanai, and MS. Mirshafiee.(In Prepration)

In this research project, we experimented with different Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Reinforcement Learning algorithms to classify fetal heartbeat sounds by gender. Sound pieces have been recorded in the third trimester of pregnancy, making it possible to extract and select features to model the problem.


Prose2Poem: The blessing of Transformer-based Language Models in translating Prose to Persian Poetry

R. Khanmohammadi, MS. Mirshafiee, YR. Jouryabi, S. A. Mirroshandel.(Under Review)

Having curated a parallel corpus of prose and their equivalent poems, we introduce a novel Neural Machine Translation (NMT) approach to translate prose to ancient Persian poetry using transformer-based Language Models in an extremely low-resource setting. More specifically, we trained a Transformer model from scratch to obtain initial translations and pretrained different variations of BERT to obtain final translations. To address the challenge of using masked language modelling under poeticness criteria, we heuristically joined the two models and generated valid poems in terms of automatic and human assessments. (A sample of one of our pretrained models on Huggingface)


COPER: a Query-adaptable Semantics-based Search Engine for Persian COVID-19 Articles

R. Khanmohammadi, MS. Mirshafiee, M. Allahyari.(DOI: 10.1109/ICWR51868.2021.9443151)
IEEE Xplore - arXiv - Video Presentation

In this research project we gathered two datasets: Persian COVID-19 Articles and PerSICK. The former consists of Healthcare articles related to COVID-19. The latter is the first Persian dataset used to measure Semantic Textual Similarity. The primary contribution of this paper is a novel Document Ranking approach which utilizez a ranking and re-ranking stage using BM25, TF-IDF, and BERT.


PGST: a Polyglot Gender Style Transfer method

R. Khanmohammadi, S. A. Mirroshandel.(Under Review)
Pre-print

In this research project, We studied linguistical and sociolinguistical differences of male/female text based on their word choices and proposed a novel approach of style transfer in gender domain. An other interesting novelty of this research was the Multilingualism of our method, which unlike other approaches, is able to be applied on multiple languages. All in all, the outcome of this research was the proposal of PGST, a Polyglot Gender Style Transfer.


Text Style Transfer in Persian Language using Deep Learning

R. Khanmohammadi, S. A. Mirroshandel.
Pre-print

In this Project, we have introduced the first instance of Text Style Transfer in Persian Language. This approach heavily relies on Greedy Search algorithm, Neural Networks, and Word Embeddings.
